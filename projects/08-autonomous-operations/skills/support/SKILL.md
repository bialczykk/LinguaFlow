# Support

## Scope Boundaries

The support department handles four categories of issues. Classify every incoming ticket into exactly one category before responding:

- **billing:** Payment failures, subscription changes, refund requests, invoice queries.
- **scheduling:** Session reschedules, cancellations, tutor availability conflicts, time zone errors.
- **tech_support:** Platform login issues, video call failures, content not loading, app bugs.
- **content_questions:** Student questions about lesson content, grammar explanations, or exercise answers.

Do not handle requests that fall outside these four categories. Redirect out-of-scope requests to the appropriate department and note the redirect in the ticket.

## Escalation Rules

Escalate a ticket to a human support manager when any of the following conditions are met:

1. The issue has been open for more than **48 hours** without resolution.
2. The student or tutor explicitly requests to speak with a human.
3. The issue involves a **billing dispute over $50** or a full refund request.
4. The same issue has been reported more than **twice** by the same student.
5. The ticket contains language indicating significant distress or a formal complaint.

When escalating, include: ticket_id, category, summary (2-3 sentences), escalation_reason, and previous_actions_taken.

## Response Templates

Use these as starting points — always personalise with the student's name and specific details:

**Billing issue acknowledged:**
"Thank you for getting in touch about your billing concern. We have flagged this for review and will update you within 1 business day. Reference: [ticket_id]."

**Scheduling change confirmed:**
"Your session on [date] has been [rescheduled/cancelled]. Your tutor [name] has been notified. Your updated schedule is reflected in the platform."

**Tech support — first response:**
"We are sorry to hear you are experiencing technical difficulties. Could you confirm: (1) the device you are using, (2) the browser/app version, and (3) the exact error message you see? This will help us resolve the issue quickly."

**Content question response:**
"Great question! [Answer to question]. If you would like further practice on this topic, your tutor can include related exercises in your next session."
